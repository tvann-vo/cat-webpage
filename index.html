Our Feline Friends

Cats have shared their lives with humans for over 10,000 years, first drawn to human settlements by the abundance of rodents. Today, they are one of the most popular pets in the world — with over 600 million domestic cats living across the globe.


From the sleek Siamese to the fluffy Maine Coon, cats come in hundreds of breeds, each with its own personality and charm. Whether they're curled up in a sunbeam or leaping after a toy, cats bring joy, comfort, and a touch of wild nature into our homes.

Fascinating Facts

  • Night vision: Cats can see in light levels 6 times lower than humans, thanks to a reflective layer behind their retinas.
  • Purring heals: A cat's purr vibrates at 25–150 Hz — frequencies known to promote bone density and tissue healing.
  • Whisker radar: Whiskers are roughly as wide as a cat's body, helping them judge whether they can fit through a space.
  • Unique nose print: Every cat's nose has a unique pattern of ridges and bumps, like a human fingerprint.
  • Righting reflex: Cats almost always land on their feet thanks to a flexible spine and an instinctive mid-air rotation.
  • Speed: Domestic cats can sprint up to 48 km/h (30 mph) in short bursts.
  • Sleep champions: Cats sleep 12–16 hours a day, conserving energy for bursts of hunting activity.
  • Ancient bond: The oldest known evidence of cat domestication dates to around 7500 BC in Cyprus.

Understanding Cat Behavior

😽

Slow Blinking

When a cat slowly closes and opens its eyes at you, it's a sign of trust and affection — often called a "cat kiss." Try returning the gesture!

🐾

Kneading

The rhythmic pushing motion cats make with their paws (also called "making biscuits") is a comforting behavior rooted in kittenhood nursing.

🎁

Bringing Gifts

When your cat brings you a "present" (toy or prey), they're treating you as part of their family and sharing their hunting success with you.

📦

Box Obsession

Cats are drawn to enclosed spaces because they feel safe and secure. Boxes reduce stress and give cats a vantage point to observe their territory.

🌙

Night Activity

Cats are crepuscular — most active at dawn and dusk. Their midnight bursts of energy are called "zoomies" and are completely normal.

💬

Meowing

Interestingly, adult cats primarily meow to communicate with humans — they rarely meow at each other. Each cat develops its own "vocabulary."
